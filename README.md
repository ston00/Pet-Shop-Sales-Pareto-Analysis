# Pareto Analysis: Customer Revenue Optimization

## 📊 Project Overview

This project implements a **Pareto analysis pipeline** using BigQuery, SQL, and Python to identify the vital few customers (the "20%") who generate the majority of revenue (the "80%"). The analysis enables data-driven business decisions on customer segmentation, resource allocation, and revenue optimization.

### Business Problem
Understanding which customers drive the most revenue is critical for:
- **Customer retention**: Focus loyalty programs on high-value customers
- **Marketing efficiency**: Allocate budgets proportionally to customer value tiers
- **Product strategy**: Tailor offerings based on top customer preferences
- **Resource planning**: Prioritize support and account management for key accounts

### Solution Approach
The pipeline uses **three complementary SQL approaches**:
1. **Views (Modular & Reusable)**: Step-by-step transformations stored as database objects
2. **CTEs (Readable & Flexible)**: Single query using Common Table Expressions for ad-hoc analysis
3. **Final Query**: Aggregated insights answering the core business question

## 📈 Core KPIs & Metrics

### **1. Revenue Metrics**

#### **Total Revenue**
- **Definition**: Sum of all sales across all customers
- **Formula**: `SUM(Quantity × Unit Price)` across entire dataset
- **Business Use**: Baseline for understanding revenue scale and growth
- **Common Questions**: "What's our total revenue this period?"

```sql
SELECT SUM(Quantity * UnitPrice) AS total_revenue
FROM sales;
```

---

#### **Customer Revenue**
- **Definition**: Total sales generated by an individual customer
- **Formula**: `SUM(Quantity × Unit Price)` grouped by CustomerID
- **Business Use**: Identify top-spenders and customer tiers
- **Common Questions**: "Who are our highest-value customers?"

```sql
SELECT CustomerID, SUM(Quantity * UnitPrice) AS customer_revenue
FROM sales
GROUP BY CustomerID
ORDER BY customer_revenue DESC;
```

---

#### **Cumulative Revenue (Cum_Revenue)**
- **Definition**: Running total of revenue when customers are ranked by value (highest to lowest)
- **Formula**: `SUM(customer_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY customer_revenue DESC ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW)`
- **Business Use**: Track aggregate revenue as you move down the customer list
- **Common Questions**: "How much revenue do the top 10 customers generate?"

```sql
SELECT 
  CustomerID,
  customer_revenue,
  SUM(customer_revenue) OVER(ORDER BY customer_revenue DESC
    ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS cum_revenue
FROM customer_sales;
```

---

### **2. Percentage & Share Metrics**

#### **Cumulative Sales Share (Cum_Sales_Share)**
- **Definition**: Percentage of total revenue captured up to the current customer
- **Formula**: `Cumulative Revenue / Total Revenue`
- **Range**: 0.0 to 1.0 (or 0% to 100%)
- **Business Use**: **Core Pareto metric** — identifies the 80% threshold
- **Common Questions**: "At what point do we hit 80% of revenue?" "How many customers generate 90% of sales?"

```sql
SELECT 
  CustomerID,
  customer_revenue,
  cum_revenue,
  total_revenue,
  cum_revenue / total_revenue AS cum_sales_share
FROM ranked_customers
ORDER BY customer_revenue DESC;
```

**Pareto Segmentation Example**:
- `cum_sales_share <= 0.80` → Vital Few (A-segment): Top customers driving majority revenue
- `0.80 < cum_sales_share <= 0.95` → Important Many (B-segment): Secondary revenue drivers
- `cum_sales_share > 0.95` → Trivial Many (C-segment): Long tail of low-value customers

---

#### **Cumulative Customer Percentage (Cum_Pct_Customer)**
- **Definition**: Percentage of total customers analyzed up to the current row
- **Formula**: `Cumulative Customer Count / Total Customers`
- **Range**: 0.0 to 1.0 (or 0% to 100%)
- **Business Use**: Answer the "80/20 question" — what % of customers drive 80% of revenue?
- **Common Questions**: "Do 20% of our customers drive 80% of revenue?" "How many customers do we need to focus on?"

```sql
SELECT 
  CustomerID,
  ROW_NUMBER() OVER(ORDER BY customer_revenue DESC) AS cum_customer,
  COUNT(*) OVER() AS total_customers,
  cum_customer / total_customers AS cum_pct_customer,
  cum_sales_share
FROM ranked_customers;
```

**Interpretation**:
- If `cum_pct_customer = 0.18` when `cum_sales_share = 0.80`, then:
  - **18% of customers drive 80% of revenue** (better than traditional 20%)
  - These are your "vital few" — candidates for premium account management

---

### **3. Count Metrics**

#### **Total Customers**
- **Definition**: Unique number of customers in the dataset
- **Formula**: `COUNT(DISTINCT CustomerID)`
- **Business Use**: Understand customer base size and concentration
- **Common Questions**: "How many total customers do we have?"

```sql
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT CustomerID) AS total_customers
FROM sales;
```

---

#### **Cumulative Customer Count (Num_of_Customers)**
- **Definition**: Number of customers needed to reach the target revenue threshold (e.g., 80%)
- **Formula**: `MIN(ROW_NUMBER)` when `cum_sales_share >= target_threshold`
- **Business Use**: Operationalize the Pareto rule — "focus on top N customers"
- **Common Questions**: "How many customers do we need to focus on to hit 80% revenue?"

```sql
SELECT 
  MIN(cum_customer) AS num_of_customers,
  MIN(cum_sales_share) AS actual_cum_sales_share
FROM ranked_customers
WHERE cum_sales_share >= 0.80;
```

**Result Example**: 
- If `num_of_customers = 42`, it means:
  - Top 42 customers generate 80%+ of total revenue
  - These 42 should be your priority for retention & growth

---

#### **Target Sales ($)**
- **Definition**: Dollar amount of revenue required to reach target threshold
- **Formula**: `Total Revenue × Target Percentage (0.80)`
- **Business Use**: Financial planning, revenue targets for key accounts
- **Common Questions**: "How much revenue do we need from top customers to hit our goals?"

```sql
SELECT 
  MIN(total_revenue * 0.80) AS target_sales,
  MIN(total_revenue) AS total_revenue
FROM ranked_customers
WHERE cum_sales_share >= 0.80;
```

---

### **4. Pareto-Specific KPIs**

#### **Pareto Threshold (80/20 Rule)**
- **Definition**: The point where cumulative customer % first exceeds cumulative revenue %
- **Calculation**: Find where `cum_sales_share >= 0.80`
- **Key Insight**: What % of customers needed to generate 80% of revenue

| Scenario | Cum_Pct_Customer | Cum_Sales_Share | Interpretation |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Healthy Pareto** | 15% | 80% | 15% of customers drive 80% of revenue (stronger concentration) |
| **Traditional 80/20** | 20% | 80% | 20% of customers drive 80% of revenue (classic Pareto) |
| **Weak Pareto** | 35% | 80% | 35% of customers drive 80% of revenue (more distributed) |

---

#### **Revenue Concentration Ratio**
- **Definition**: How concentrated is revenue among top customers?
- **Calculation**: `Total Revenue of Top 20% / Total Revenue`
- **Range**: 0.0 to 1.0
- **Business Use**: Assess business risk — high concentration = customer dependency risk
- **Common Questions**: "Are we too dependent on a few customers?"

```sql
SELECT 
  ROUND(SUM(customer_revenue) / MAX(total_revenue), 2) AS top_20pct_concentration
FROM ranked_customers
WHERE cum_pct_customer <= 0.20;
```

**Risk Assessment**:
- `> 0.70` → High concentration (risky: losing key accounts is devastating)
- `0.50-0.70` → Moderate concentration (manageable risk)
- `< 0.50` → Diversified base (lower risk but possibly untapped high-value accounts)

---

#### **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) - Derived**
- **Definition**: Total revenue contributed by a customer (in context of this analysis)
- **Formula**: `SUM(Quantity × Unit Price)` per customer
- **Business Use**: Segment customers by value, tailor retention strategies
- **Common Questions**: "Rank customers by lifetime value"

```sql
SELECT 
  CustomerID,
  SUM(Quantity * UnitPrice) AS customer_lifetime_value,
  ROW_NUMBER() OVER(ORDER BY SUM(Quantity * UnitPrice) DESC) AS rank
FROM sales
GROUP BY CustomerID;
```

## 🔍 SQL Implementation Details

### **Approach 1: Views (Modular)**
**Best For**: Production pipelines, multiple stakeholders, maintaining consistency

```
sales_v1: Calculate sales per transaction
    ↓
sales_v2: Aggregate by customer
    ↓
sales_v3: Rank & calculate cumulative metrics
    ↓
Final Query: Extract Pareto insights
```

**Advantages**:
- Reusable across multiple queries
- Easy to audit and maintain
- Clear separation of concerns
- Enables incremental improvements

---

### **Approach 2: CTEs (Flexible)**
**Best For**: Ad-hoc analysis, rapid prototyping, single complex queries

```sql
WITH base_sales AS (...),
     customer_sales AS (...),
     ranked AS (...),
     with_pct AS (...)
SELECT ... FROM with_pct WHERE cum_sales_share >= 0.80;
```

**Advantages**:
- Self-contained, no external dependencies
- Easier to test and iterate
- More readable step-by-step logic
- No database permissions needed for view creation

---

### **Approach 3: Final Query (Aggregated)**
**Best For**: Executive dashboards, summary reports

Extracts the "headline numbers":
- Number of customers to focus on
- Their cumulative revenue
- Actual vs. target revenue share
- Customer percentage

## 📊 Common Business Questions & SQL Solutions

### Q1: "Who are our top 20 customers?"
```sql
SELECT TOP 20
  CustomerID,
  customer_revenue,
  ROUND(100 * customer_revenue / total_revenue, 2) AS pct_of_total_revenue
FROM sales_v2
ORDER BY customer_revenue DESC;
```

### Q2: "How many customers generate 80% of revenue?"
```sql
SELECT 
  MIN(cum_customer) AS num_of_customers,
  MIN(cum_sales_share) AS cum_sales_share,
  ROUND(100 * MIN(cum_pct_customer), 2) AS pct_of_total_customers
FROM sales_v3
WHERE cum_sales_share >= 0.80;
```

### Q3: "What's the average revenue per customer in each Pareto segment?"
```sql
SELECT 
  CASE 
    WHEN cum_sales_share <= 0.80 THEN 'A (Vital Few)'
    WHEN cum_sales_share <= 0.95 THEN 'B (Important Many)'
    ELSE 'C (Trivial Many)'
  END AS segment,
  COUNT(*) AS num_customers,
  ROUND(AVG(customer_revenue), 2) AS avg_revenue,
  ROUND(SUM(customer_revenue), 2) AS total_segment_revenue
FROM sales_v3
GROUP BY segment
ORDER BY total_segment_revenue DESC;
```

### Q4: "Are we over-dependent on a few customers (concentration risk)?"
```sql
SELECT 
  SUM(CASE WHEN cum_pct_customer <= 0.20 THEN customer_revenue ELSE 0 END) AS top_20pct_revenue,
  SUM(customer_revenue) AS total_revenue,
  ROUND(100 * SUM(CASE WHEN cum_pct_customer <= 0.20 THEN customer_revenue ELSE 0 END) 
    / SUM(customer_revenue), 2) AS top_20pct_concentration_pct
FROM sales_v3;
```

### Q5: "How is revenue distributed? (Gini Coefficient for inequality)"
```sql
-- Advanced: Gini coefficient shows distribution equality (0=equal, 1=total inequality)
WITH ranked_revenue AS (
  SELECT 
    customer_revenue,
    ROW_NUMBER() OVER(ORDER BY customer_revenue) AS rank,
    COUNT(*) OVER() AS total_count,
    SUM(customer_revenue) OVER() AS total_revenue
  FROM sales_v2
)
SELECT 
  ROUND(
    (2 * SUM(rank * customer_revenue) / (total_count * total_revenue)) - 
    ((total_count + 1) / total_count),
    3
  ) AS gini_coefficient
FROM ranked_revenue;
-- Gini > 0.70 = high inequality (Pareto-driven)
-- Gini < 0.40 = more equal distribution
```
